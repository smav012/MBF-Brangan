58A Dangerousness Hearings

  • Type: Mechanism
  • Secondary Types: Preventative Detention
  • 7 relationships

    • Current Legislative Initiatives

    Bill H. 4290 (2021-22) An Act To Protect Victims of Crime and the Public --> would expand pre-trial detention related to dangerousness.

    Potential Solutions

    New Jersey Criminal Justice Reform Act (RTF pg 15)

    California SB10 (RTF pg 26): Similar to others, increased use of RATs while promising elimination of money bail. RATs would increase the power and funding of the PIC while also perpetuating racist and classist algorithms. This intervention is an example of trying to take out a ""tangential element of the PIC"" without reducing state or prison power.

    Timeline/Legislative History

    • Commonwealth v. R.P. (1974) – SJC ruling establishing judicial power to consider dangerousness when making bail decisions.
    • M.G.L. c. 276, §58A: Dangerousness Hearing Statute (1994) – judge can hold someone deemed "dangerous" for up to 120 days before trial.
    • Massachusetts General Laws – Offenses under 58A.
    • NEW SINCE AUGUST 2024: assault with a dangerous weapon (ADW) can be charged under 58A.
