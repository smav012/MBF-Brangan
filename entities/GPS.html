MBF Brangan Project

GPS Monitoring

  • Type: Mechanism
  • Secondary Types: Electronic Monitoring
  • 4 relationships

    • Funding

    $350,000 allocated towards “increased electronic monitoring capacity” in FY2024; unclear if for all probation or pre-trial services.

    Current Legislative Initiatives

    HD.1670 / SD.1033 (2023-2024): penalties for tampering with GPS devices; paired with SD.1032 / S.1098 (felony, minimum 2 years imprisonment). Last actions: 8/8/24 & H.4989/HD.2482 comprehensive bill draft.

    Potential Solutions

    Illinois pre-trial GPS law (720 ILCS 5/110-5 & 730 ILCS 5/5-8A-4): prosecutors prove need, judges reconsider every 60 days, limited freedom restrictions, credit for time served while monitored.

    4 relationships

    1. Authorized by - Commissioner of Probation

      Commissioner of Probation sets policy and authorizes use of electronic monitoring.

    2. Enforced by - ELMO Unit

      ELMO Unit enforces GPS monitoring conditions and responds to violations.

    3. Overseen by - Parole Board

      Parole Board may impose GPS monitoring as a condition of parole.

    4. Monitored by - Probation Officers

      Probation Officers monitor compliance with GPS conditions and report violations.

    Timeline/Legislative History

    • MPS introduces GPS monitoring April 2001 for probationers.
    • M.G.L. c. 276, §58: amended 2006
    • M.G.L. c. 265 §47 (2006):requires GPS for sex offender probationers.
    • Commonwealth v. Guzman (2014): GPS as condition doesn't violate due process.
    • Commonwealth v. Grundman (2016): special case GPS during sentencing not imposed.
    • Commonwealth v. Feliz (2019): blanket GPS monitoring violates right to privacy.
    • Commonwealth v. Norman (2020): limits pre-trial GPS monitoring power.
