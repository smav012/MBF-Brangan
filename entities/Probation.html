Probation

Type: Institution

Definition

Probation is the supervisory institution responsible for enforcing court-ordered conditions of release, monitoring compliance, and coordinating with other justice entities and service providers. It operates under the authority of the Commissioner of Probation (Executive Office) and interacts with multiple decision makers and mechanisms to manage pretrial and post-conviction supervision.

Mechanisms (conditions of release)

  • Community Justice Support Centers
  • Community Service
  • Drug Testing
  • GPS Monitoring
  • Probation Violations
  • SCRAM Monitoring

Primary Decision Makers

  • Commissioner of Probation: executive oversight.
  • Probation Officers: day-to-day supervision, enforcement of conditions.
  • ELMO Unit: electronic monitoring administration.

External/Interacting Decision Makers

  • Bail Magistrate: bail hearings, decisions tied to pretrial supervision.
  • Clerks: scheduling, notifications, recordkeeping.
  • CJIS/Police Records Departments: supply background info and criminal records.
  • Defense Counsel: legal representation.
  • Drug Courts: coordinate treatment-based probation conditions.
  • Drug Testing Companies: provide results for supervision compliance.
  • Massachusetts Sentencing Commission: oversight, data, policy input.
  • Parole Board: oversight for post-conviction supervised release.
  • Police Departments: enforce warrants, detain on default.
  • Prosecutor: file motions or intervene when supervision violations occur.
  • Reentry Subcommittee: provide community support, programmatic coordination.
  • Warrant Management Unit (WMU): manage default warrants.
