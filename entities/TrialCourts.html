Trial Courts

Type: Institution

Definition

Trial Courts are the judicial institutions responsible for conducting trials, hearing cases, and making initial legal determinations.

Mechanisms

  • 58A Dangerousness Hearings
  • Bail Forfeitures
  • Bail Revocations
  • Defaults and Warrants (Willful Flight)
  • Section 35
  • Sec 58/Cash Bail and Personal Recognizance

Primary Decision Makers

  • Judges: preside over cases, issue rulings, authorizations, and warrants.
  • Clerks: manage court records, schedule hearings, process filings and motions.
  • Bail Magistrate: conduct bail hearings, make pretrial decisions.

External/Interacting Decision Makers

  • Probation Officers: report compliance or violations of supervised release.
  • ELMO Unit: provide electronic monitoring information for court consideration.
  • Drug Testing Companies: supply test results for court supervision hearings.
  • CJIS/Police Records Departments: provide background and criminal record information.
  • Police Departments: enforce court orders, detain individuals on warrants.
  • Warrant Management Unit (WMU): manage default warrants issued by the court.
  • Defense Counsel: provide legal representation for defendants.
  • Prosecutor: file motions, respond to violations, and support court processes.
  • Parole Board: oversee post-conviction supervised release considerations.
  • Drug Courts: implement treatment-based probation conditions.
  • Massachusetts Sentencing Commission: provide policy guidance, data, and oversight.
  • Reentry Subcommittee: coordinate community support and reintegration programs.
