MBF Brangan Project

Defaults and Warrants (Willful Flight)

  • Type: Mechanism
  • Secondary Types: Court Enforcement/Compliance
  • 5 relationships

    • Funding

    No funding for the program directly but $300,000 to maintain text message system of court reminders.

    Current Legislative Initiatives

    HD.3330 - Michael S. Day (same bill H.4322 in Joint Committee on the Judiciary). Been in the works for a few years now, since 2020. Expands scope of who counts as probation officer and therefore who can arrest (without warrant) probationers. Last action 3/7/24 (Read second and ordered to a third reading)

    Potential Solutions

    Kansas City, Missouri: Walk-in court that removes possibility of arrest for people trying to resolve warrants. Walk-in dockets language changed to state that people will not be arrested when they show up voluntarily to clear an appearance warrant. Expanded walk-in docket schedule and added legal aids (expansion of system). Trying to get a function to let people schedule their own court date.

    5 relationships

    1. Authorized by – Judges

      Judges declare defaults and issue warrants for failure to appear.

    2. Enforced by - Warrant Management Unit (WMU)

      WMU issues, tracks, and manages default warrants authorized by the court.

    3. Enforced by - Police Departments

      Police execute default warrants by holding and transporting individuals.

    4. Administered by - Clerks

      Clerks manage court appearance records, process default paperwork, and update bail records.

    5. Monitored by - Probation Officers

      Probation monitors compliance with release conditions and may report violations leading to defaults.

    Timeline/Legislative History
