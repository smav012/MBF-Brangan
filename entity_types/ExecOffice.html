Criminal Courts (Executive Office)

Type: Institution

Definition

Primary Decision Makers

  • Commissioner of Probation (Edward J Dolan): oversees all probation operations, policy, and executive management.
  • Clerks: manage administrative court functions, records, and notifications under the Exec. Office.

External/Interacting Decision Makers

  • Probation Officers: report supervision and compliance info, carry out directives from Exec. Office.
  • ELMO Unit: provide electronic monitoring services used for oversight and enforcement.
  • Drug Testing Companies: provide results for supervision compliance.
  • CJIS/Police Records Departments: supply criminal record info to support decision-making.
  • Police Departments: coordinate enforcement actions in support of probation or court orders.
  • Warrant Management Unit (WMU): manage warrants issued or tracked under Exec. Office supervision.
  • Bail Magistrate: acts on pretrial matters that intersect with executive policy.
  • Judges: provide formal authorization and rulings that guide Exec. Office operations.
  • Defense Counsel: interacts indirectly through procedural compliance and legal representation.
  • Prosecutor: engages when enforcement actions or motions arise from Exec. Office directives.
  • Parole Board: coordinates post-conviction supervision where policies overlap.
  • Drug Courts: operate under the larger supervision framework of the Exec. Office.
  • Massachusetts Sentencing Commission: provides oversight, policy input, and data analytics.
  • Reentry Subcommittee: advises and coordinates community-based reintegration programs.
